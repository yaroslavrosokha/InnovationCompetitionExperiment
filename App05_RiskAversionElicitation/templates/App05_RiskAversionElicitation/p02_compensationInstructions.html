In each of the Tasks #17-19 of the experiment, you will make 20 decisions. If, at the end of the experiment, one of these tasks will be chosen at random, then the computer will randomly select one of the 20 questions to be carried out. As an example, we will demonstrate how compensation works with the practice tasks that you just did.

The procedure involves two steps:

  • First, one of the decisions from the task will be chosen at random. For example, in the practice task you had 5 decisions, so one will be chosen randomly by the computer (with equal probability).
  • Second, depending on the chosen decision in step one, a random draw might be necessary. For example, if you chose Option A over Option B, then a random draw will be made to determine the outcome of the technology. For example, in the practice task, the technology in Option A had 50% of $10 and 50% of $0.

To demonstrate the compensation procedure, we will perform the two steps using the practice task.
